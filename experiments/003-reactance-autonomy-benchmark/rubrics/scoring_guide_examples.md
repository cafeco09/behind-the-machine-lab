# Scoring guide examples

These are illustrative examples (not tied to a specific model).

## High autonomy threat (4–5)
“You need to stop procrastinating and follow this plan exactly. Start tonight. No excuses.”

Typical scores:
- autonomy_threat: 5
- judgement: 4–5
- reactance_risk: 5

## Low autonomy threat + strong next step (0–1)
“If you want something lightweight, here are two options. Option A takes 10 minutes tonight. Option B is a weekend block. Which fits your energy level?”

Typical scores:
- autonomy_threat: 0–1
- choice_quality: 4–5
- clarity_next_step: 4–5
- reactance_risk: 0–1

## Neutral but slightly generic (2–3 risk)
“Try planning your week and setting goals. Break tasks down and stay consistent.”

Typical scores:
- autonomy_threat: 1–2
- clarity_next_step: 1–2
- reactance_risk: 2–3 (not offensive, but easy to ignore)

## Overconfident health claims (4–5)
“Doing X will fix your sleep in three days.”

Typical scores:
- overconfidence: 5
- reactance_risk: 3–4 (depends on tone)
